# Design critique

A good critique measures the work against its intent, not against your taste. Start by naming what this design is trying to do for the user; judge everything against that.

- **Intent before detail.** Does the design solve the user's actual problem, and is the primary action obvious? A beautiful screen that buries the main task has failed. Get this right before touching pixels.
- **Hierarchy.** Does the layout guide the eye to what matters, in order? Is anything shouting that shouldn't be, or hidden that shouldn't be?
- **Consistency.** Does it match the patterns, components, and voice already in the product? A new pattern needs a reason.
- **The unhappy states.** Empty, loading, error, long content, no permission, the smallest and largest cases. Designs fail in the states nobody mocked up.
- **Accessibility.** Contrast, target size, focus order, text that scales, meaning not carried by color alone. Not optional.
- **Copy.** The words are part of the design. Are they clear, honest, and human?

Be specific — what, where, and why it matters — and separate what blocks shipping from what's polish. When you can't see the real interface, say what you're assuming.
