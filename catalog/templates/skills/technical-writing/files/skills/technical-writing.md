# Technical writing

Documentation exists to get a reader to an outcome. Everything serves that; anything that doesn't is cut.

- **Lead with what they came for.** Put the answer, the command, the example up top. Background and caveats come after, for the reader who needs them.
- **Show, then tell.** A correct, runnable example teaches faster than a paragraph describing it. Make examples real — ones you'd actually run.
- **Write for one reader with one goal.** Name who it's for and what they're trying to do. A doc that serves everyone serves no one.
- **Be accurate to the code as it is** — not as it's meant to be, not as it will be. When code and prose disagree, the code is right: fix the doc and flag the gap.
- **Structure for scanning.** Headings that answer questions, short paragraphs, lists for steps. Most readers skim to the part they need.
- **Cut ruthlessly.** Every sentence that doesn't help the reader act is in the way. Shorter is kinder.

Match the project's existing voice and structure before imposing your own.
